Disease Prevention Measures

Asthma

  • Avoid exposure to known triggers such as tobacco smoke, dust, pet dander, and air pollution.

  • Follow prescribed medication and treatment plans.

  • Maintain a clean indoor environment by regularly cleaning and dusting.

  • Practice good respiratory hygiene, including proper handwashing and covering your mouth when coughing or sneezing.

  • Consider allergen-proof covers for mattresses and pillows.

Dengue Fever

  • Eliminate stagnant water where mosquitoes breed by regularly emptying containers, flower pots, and other items that collect water.

  • Use mosquito repellents and wear long-sleeved clothing in areas prone to dengue.

  • Install screens on windows and doors to prevent mosquitoes from entering living spaces.

  • Seek medical care immediately if experiencing symptoms to prevent complications.

  • Community efforts for mosquito control and sanitation measures can also help prevent the spread of dengue.

Tuberculosis

  • Get vaccinated if available in your region.

  • Practice respiratory hygiene by covering your mouth when coughing or sneezing.

  • Avoid close contact with individuals with active TB.

  • Ensure proper ventilation in indoor spaces.

  • Finish the entire course of prescribed medication if diagnosed with TB to prevent drug resistance.

Lung Cancer

  • Avoid tobacco in any form, including smoking and secondhand smoke exposure.

  • Minimize exposure to environmental carcinogens like radon, asbestos, and certain pollutants.

  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle with a balanced diet and regular exercise.

  • Consider regular health check-ups and screenings, especially for high-risk individuals (e.g., smokers).

  • Follow workplace safety guidelines and use protective equipment if exposed to hazardous substances.

Hypertensive Heart Disease

  • Adopt a heart-healthy lifestyle with a balanced diet and regular exercise.

  • Manage stress levels effectively through relaxation techniques and mindfulness practices.

  • Regularly monitor blood pressure and follow prescribed medications as directed by healthcare providers.

  • Limit salt intake and maintain a healthy weight.

  • Avoid excessive alcohol consumption and tobacco use.

Kidney Cancer

  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle by eating a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.

  • Stay hydrated by drinking an adequate amount of water daily.

  • Quit smoking and avoid exposure to secondhand smoke.

  • Maintain a healthy weight and exercise regularly.

  • Limit exposure to environmental toxins and carcinogens whenever possible.

Diabetes

  • Follow a balanced diet with appropriate portion sizes and avoid excessive consumption of sugary foods and beverages.

  • Engage in regular physical activity to maintain a healthy weight and improve insulin sensitivity.

  • Monitor blood sugar levels regularly, especially if at risk for diabetes, and follow medical advice.

  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption.

  • Manage stress effectively through relaxation techniques and adequate sleep.

Influenza

  • Get vaccinated annually against seasonal flu strains.

  • Practice good hygiene, including frequent handwashing with soap and water or using hand sanitizer.

  • Avoid close contact with sick individuals and stay home if you're feeling unwell.

  • Cover your mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing to prevent the spread of droplets.

  • Keep commonly touched surfaces clean and disinfected, especially during flu seasons.

HIV/AIDS

  • Practice safe sex by using condoms to reduce the risk of contracting or spreading HIV.

  • Get tested regularly for HIV and other sexually transmitted infections (STIs).

  • Avoid sharing needles or any equipment that may be contaminated with blood.

  • Consider pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) for individuals at high risk of HIV.

  • Educate oneself and others about HIV transmission and prevention to reduce stigma and promote awareness.

Hypertension (High Blood Pressure):

  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle by following a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains, and low in saturated fats and cholesterol.

  • Engage in regular physical activity and exercise most days of the week.

  • Reduce sodium intake by avoiding high-sodium processed foods and adding less salt to meals.

  • Limit alcohol consumption and avoid smoking or tobacco use.

  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques, meditation, or other stress-reducing activities.
